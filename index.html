Important Details

Welcome to our wedding website! Thank you for being a part of our celebration. This is the place where you'll find every piece of information you will need regarding our wedding celebration. This will be updated regularly.


Our wedding will be taking place in beautiful Indore, Madhya Pradesh, on January 8, 2018. The celebrations will begin from January 6, 2018. Nikita was born and brought up in Indore and we couldn't think of a better place to celebrate this special occasion than the place where she celebrated 20 years of her life. So mark your calendars because we would love for you to come and celebrate with us!


We look forward to celebrating our wedding with as many friends and family members as possible, but we realize that a trip to Indore is not a possibility for some. For those who cannot make the trip, we will have a reception and celebration in Mumbai, Maharashtra, on January 13, 2018 so that we can share our joy with all of you.

Sweet love story

August 2013

We first met in the Fall of 2013 in Atlanta, Georgia. It seems like yesterday when he saw an ambitious girl sitting in front of him in the Applied Cryptography class at Georgia Tech. Mutual friends introduced us and soon we became project partners. During the difficult time of struggling between classes, project deadlines and adjusting in a new country, our friendship grew stronger. Little did we know that that was the beginning of our forever lasting friendship. We went from project partners to being friends to best friends, and today we are getting married. We can not feel more grateful that everything came together for us and with the blessings from both the families we are about to take the marriage vows. Although our journey may not have begun like a fairy tale, we know we are destined for a story book ending.

The wedding

Accommodation for guests at The Exotica, Indore

Ganesh Pooja (Bride's side)

  • Before the ceremony begins, the Priest invokes Lord Ganesh, the Hindu elephant God that removes all obstacles, as a precursor to nuptials about to take place. This ceremony is performed for good luck to be bestowed onto the married couple and their families, so that whatever obstacles they may face will be destroyed.
  • Saturday, 6 January 2018 (Morning)
  • The Exotica, Indore

Dinner meet and mingle with karaoke

  • An informal meetup of the guests from both sides of the families for a fun and deliciously appetizing dinner, complete with an on-demand Karaoke setup, for you to show off your awesome singing skills.
  • Saturday, 6 January 2018 (Evening)
  • The Exotica, Indore

Haldi

  • The haldi ceremony is one in which a paste of turmeric is applied on the bride and the groom’s body before their wedding by their near and dear ones. The paste serves as a cleanser for the body and soul and signifies the would be couple's preparation and welcoming into married life. This ceremony is also accompanied by traditional songs and dances.
  • Sunday, 7 January 2018 (Morning)
  • The Exotica, Indore

Dev Devak (Groom's side)

  • Divine blessings are sought from the family deity. Each of the families has one particular deity they worship and the worship of this family deity is called 'Devkarya'. The bride and her parents, and the groom and his parents, perform this ritual separately.
  • Monday, 7 January 2018 (Morning)
  • The Exotica, Indore

Mehendi

  • The beautiful bride-to-be adorns her hands and feet with striking designs made out of henna. Mehendi represents the bond of matrimony and is therefore, considered a sign of good luck. It signifies the love and affection between the couple and their families. This ceremony is also accompanied by traditional songs and dances. Men, while the women enjoy this ceremony, we have a special event planned for you.
  • Sunday, 7 January 2018 (Morning)
  • The Exotica, Indore

Sangeet

  • This ceremony is a time for celebrating, singing, dancing and joking, and is a perfect time for wedding guests to meet and get to know each other. So put on your dancing shoes and hit the floor!
  • Sunday, 7 January 2018 (Evening)
  • The Exotica, Indore

Engagement

  • The engagement ceremony is an important pre-wedding ritual in Indian cultures where the would-be-bride and the bridegroom-to-be come face to face and are formally betrothed to each other by their families. It involves the groom's family giving their words to the bride's family that they welcome their daughter and will be responsible for her happiness and wellbeing.
  • Sunday, 7 January 2018 (Evening)
  • The Exotica, Indore

Bhat, Karpani, Milni (Bride's side)

  • The significance of the Milni Ceremony is that it showcases the acceptance of the marriage and the bond that the families have created. The Bride's side welcome the Groom and his family by exchanging flower garlands, symbolizing the bonding of the two families.
  • Monday, 8 January 2018 (Morning)
  • The Exotica, Indore

Reception, followed by Wedding

  • The big day! The evening starts off with a 'baarat' procession of the groom's side dancing their way to the wedding venue, where the groom is welcomed by the bride's family. A wedding reception follows with dinner for all guests, following which the formal wedding ceremony begins later in the night.
  • Monday, 8 January 2018 (Evening)
  • The Exotica, Indore

Vidai

  • Literally translating to the phrase 'Goodbye', Vidai is one of the most sentimental outburst of any wedding across the world. The last ritual of the wedding, this Indian ceremony marks the begining of the bride's new role as a wife and daughter-in-law.
  • Tuesday, 9 January 2018 (Early morning)
  • The Exotica, Indore

Mumbai Reception

  • A second wedding reception, this time in the groom's city of residence, is a celebration occasion for all well wishers, primarily those that couldn't make it to the wedding destination.
  • Saturday, 13 January 2018 (Evening)
  • Juhu Club Millenium, Mumbai
